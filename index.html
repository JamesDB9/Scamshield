Types of common scams you may come across

As the world has progressed into a society dependant on technology, many malicious threat actors have invented new ways to exploit people and steal from you. Whilst there are an abundance of scams that are being performed on a daily basis, some of the most common scams are listed.

About us

Email + Phone Scams

Scammers often use email, text messages, phone calls and social media. In order to steal your information, scammers will pretend to be someone you trust and then abuse that trust to gain information that would otherwise be safe. It is important to be vigilant and be observent when you recieve a suspicious email or text message, even if you can trust the sender.

Targeted Phising Scams / Spear Phising

These attacks are a common type of phishing attack in which specific targetted individuals or organizations typically through malicious emails or other contact methods. A scammer who is using a spear phising attack would have the goal of stealing private and sensitive information for example, login credentials or infect the targets' device with malware to try and steal even more information.

Hoax websites

When logging in to banking, shopping or email sites, always look for “https” at the beginning of the URL – the “s” stands for secure. Check for the padlock symbol in your browser’s address bar. REMEMBER! Hoax websites often look like the real thing so make sure to be vigilant.

Family Impersonation "Hi Mum" Scams

Whilst the 'Hi Mum' text scam is simple, it is extremely effective. Scammers will often send a text message or call you, usually via WhatsApp or SMS, posing as a friend or family member. Often, the family member will claim to have lost their phone or got a new number. Then, the scammer can claim to be locked out of their online banking system, or be in some kind of trouble and ask the victim to send money.
